What is Portugal famous for?

The country is famous for a large number of ecologically clean beaches - they are marked with a blue flag. In 2019, there were 352 of them: 317 on the ocean coast and 35 on the river banks. In terms of security, Portugal ranks second among European countries. Portugal is rich in large and small freshwater reservoirs with a large number of fish species. Ocean fishing is the most popular: medium-sized fish can be caught all year round, even from the shore, and tuna and marlin can be caught in open water from September.

The main sectors of the Portuguese economy : clothing and footwear production; woodworking, paper and cork production; metal processing; oil refining and chemical; fish processing; wine and port production; tourism. Life in Portugal has many advantages: warm climate, ocean and mountains nearby, low crime rate, relatively low cost of living . Medicine and education are well developed here, and residents are offered preferential tax treatment. Disadvantages include the need to learn the language and carry cash.
